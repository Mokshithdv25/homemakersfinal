## 2. The opportunity (why this wins now)

*Summary of the research pack in `~/Documents/design-compass/docs/business_implementation_plan.md` — full source list is in that file.*

### Who we serve (ICP)

- **Not** only the **~5–10%** who want a **fully managed** interior / turn-key player (Livspace-style).
- **We build for the ~82–90%** who work with **independent architects, contractors, and local builders**, want **control**, but today run on **WhatsApp + cash + vague scope** — no locked estimate, no real project OS.

### Macro (India) — cited ranges from the research pack

| Signal | Order of magnitude | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|--------|
| India total construction (2024) | **~$687B** | See business plan for sources (Research and Markets, BusinessWire, etc.) |
| India **residential** construction (2024) | **~$190B** | GlobeNewWire et al. |
| Residential (2025, projected) | **~$264B–$404B** | Mordor Intelligence, Research and Markets |
| Interior design market (2024) | **~$24B–$36B** | Credence, TechSci, VMR |
| Home improvement (2024) | **~$34.7B** | Deep Market Insights |
| Home remodeling (2024) | **~$12.3B** | Deep Market Insights |
| Homes renovating annually (2024) | **~5 million** | Ken Research |
| Online penetration of home services | **~0.8%** | HDFC TRU IPO docs — **massive headroom** |

### Structural reality (why software should exist)

- **~90%+** of construction workers in the **unorganized** layer; **~82%** of residential market is **small, fragmented builders** — the system is **relationships + WhatsApp**, not a product.
- **400M+** WhatsApp users in India — coordination already lives in chat; **HomeMakers** turns that chaos into **locked scope, estimates, and a project room**.

### Honest monetizable layer (not “we capture all of construction”)

From the same plan: we target the **decision + coordination** layer — on the order of **3–8%** of project value. On a **~$35B–$50B** renovation + interior envelope, the **serviceable** layer is on the order of **~$1.5B–$4B** (conservative **~$1.5B–$2.5B**). **Enough for a category company if we own the layer** — without claiming fake “trillion-TAM” ownership of every brick.

### Why now

- **Affordability:** new homes vs income, **renovation over new buy** (plan cites price vs income gap, affordable stock down — see master doc).
- **AI + structured data** can deliver **clarity at scale** without armies of estimators.
- **Competitors** are strong in **managed interiors** or **discovery** — not in a **pre-execution OS** for the long tail.

**Pitch cheat sheet (vs. names investors throw at you):** [COMPETITIVE_POSITIONING.md](./COMPETITIVE_POSITIONING.md) — **Material Depot** = materials retail; **Room Story AI** = image → product buy; **Livspace / Homelane** = end-to-end operator; **HomeMakers** = AI v0 + open pro marketplace + project management.

## 3. The product (what we’re building)

- **For families:** New build + remodel **guided flows** (plot, Vastu, materials, budget in lakhs/crores — **built for India**), **AI-assisted** planning, then a **project hub** — tasks, documents, team, **marketplace** for pros — *one place instead of 40 chats.*
- **For design & build professionals:** **Portfolio and identity** on platform, **one link** to clients, future **leads and marketplace** surface (roadmap: RFQ, compare quotes, **budget linked to work** per `docs/PROJECT_MANAGEMENT_ROADMAP.md`).
- **Why us vs. generic “AI for X”:** We’re not a wrapper — we own **onboarding data model + project graph + pro surface**; **that’s the moat** when you add **payments, trust, and network**.
